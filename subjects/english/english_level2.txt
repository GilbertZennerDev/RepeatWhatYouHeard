**1. The Conditional Dilemma (Type 1 & 2):** Complete the following two sentences using the correct verb forms for the **First Conditional** (Type 1) and the **Second Conditional** (Type 2):
* If I **win** (win) the lottery, I \_\_\_\_\_ (buy) a house.
* If I **were** (be) rich, I \_\_\_\_\_ (travel) the world.
**=END=**

**2. Phrasal Verb Replacement:** Replace the bolded word in each of the following four sentences with a common **Phrasal Verb** that has a similar meaning (*look up, turn down, call off, take off*):
* We had to **cancel** the meeting.
* I will **search for** the word in the dictionary.
* The plane will **depart** soon.
* She **rejected** the offer.
**=END=**

**3. The Passive Transformation (Different Tenses):** Transform the following two active sentences into the **Passive Voice**, using the correct tense for each:
* The cleaner **cleans** the rooms every day (Present Simple).
* They **will finish** the project tomorrow (Future Simple).
**=END=**

**4. Reported Speech Nuance:** Change the following direct speech into **reported speech**. Pay attention to the necessary shift in tense and pronoun:
* He said, "I **am working** on the report right now."
* She asked, "Where **did** you go yesterday?"
**=END=**

**5. Relative Clause Connection:** Combine the following two pairs of sentences into one complex sentence using a suitable **Relative Pronoun** (*who, which, whose, that*):
* This is the book. I told you about the book.
* That is the student. Her mother is a doctor.
**=END=**

**6. Gerund or Infinitive:** Complete the following three sentences by choosing the correct form (**Gerund** or **Infinitive**):
* I enjoy \_\_\_\_\_ (read) novels.
* She decided \_\_\_\_\_ (study) medicine.
* They avoid \_\_\_\_\_ (talk) about politics.
**=END=**

**7. Modal Verb for Deduction:** Write three different sentences about a dark sky and an approaching sound. Use a different **Modal Verb** in each sentence to express a different level of **deduction** or **certainty** about what is happening (*must, might, can't*).
**=END=**

**8. Advanced Comparative:** Write two sentences comparing two objects (A and B). Use an **Adverb** (*quickly, well, carefully*) in both sentences. One sentence must use the structure ***as... as***, and the other must use the structure ***more/less... than***.
**=END=**

**9. The Prepositional Phrase Opener:** Write three descriptive sentences about a library. Each sentence must begin with a **Prepositional Phrase** that describes either time, location, or manner.
**=END=**

**10. Future Perfect vs. Continuous:** Use the provided time markers to write two distinct sentences for the same action (*study*), applying the correct future tense for each:
* By next week, I \_\_\_\_\_ (Future Perfect).
* This time tomorrow, I \_\_\_\_\_ (Future Continuous).
**=END=**
